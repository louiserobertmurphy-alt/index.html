The concept

One big question about education, careers or ambition per edition. Two answers — one from a Vice-Chancellor who's spent 30+ years at the top of creative industries and higher education, one from a coach sitting with students through 2,000+ personal statements. They don't always agree. That's the point.

The format in one sentence: One question. One answer each. Where they agree, where they don't, and one practical thing readers can do with it.

Why it works

What they bringWhy it matters
Olivier: top-down, 30-year career view — hired, led, built institutionsAuthority and long-horizon thinking. The "so what does this mean for your career?" angle.
Louise: ground-level, current — inside the personal statement process right nowPractical, specific, immediately useful. The angle parents and students are looking for.
Father & daughter — genuinely different viewsThey disagree. That makes it worth reading. No corporate newsletter vibes.
Two audiences, one newsletterHE leaders & execs find Olivier. Students & parents find Louise. Both subscribe.

Format & workflow

Edition structure

ElementLengthNotes
Intro + the question~60 wordsHook immediately. The question is the headline. One provocative sentence max.
Louise's angle200–250 wordsGround-level, specific. Always tied to something she's seen in real applications.
Olivier's angle200–250 wordsStrategic, long-view. Draws on career and institutional experience.
Where we agree / don't60–80 wordsBe honest. If they genuinely agree, say so briefly. Disagreement gets two sentences each.
What we'd recommend50–70 wordsOne actionable thing. Should be specific enough to do this week.
Optional coda~40 wordsA book, a stat, a resource, or a TikTok embed where relevant.

Fortnightly workflow

Week 1 · Mon–Tue

Agree the question

  • Pick from the bank or surface something current
  • Louise writes rough angle (≤300 words, unpolished)
Week 1 · Wed–Thu

Olivier writes, both review

  • Olivier drafts his angle independently (no reading Louise's first)
  • Share both drafts — let the disagreement emerge naturally
Week 1 · Fri

Write the closing section together

  • The "agree/disagree" section is the only part written jointly
  • The recommendation must be specific — reject anything vague
Week 2 · Mon

Final edit + schedule

  • Louise leads the final copy edit for consistency
  • Schedule for Tuesday or Thursday, 8–9am UK time
  • Prep TikTok teaser clip if relevant
Week 2 · Publish day

Publish + engage

  • Both post a personal LinkedIn post linking to it — different framing, not identical
  • Both respond to every comment for the first 72 hours
  • Louise: TikTok clip pulling one key moment from the edition

Writing rules (non-negotiable)

Start with the disagreement, not the consensus. · One idea per section — no lists dressed up as insight. · First draft: too long. Cut 30%. Publish the third version. · The closing recommendation must be doable this week, not "consider exploring..." · Funny where possible. This is a dinner table argument, not a thought leadership piece. · Never blend the voices — Olivier top-down, Louise ground-up, always.

The newsletter is one of three distribution channels for Loulou Learning (alongside TikTok and LinkedIn). The goal is to convert readers into paying clients for 1:1 and group coaching.

1:1 PS coachingUCAS personal statement review, drafting support, strategy — UK, European, US applicationsActive

Infrastructure checklist

  • Beacons.ai landing page — link hub: about, services, booking, newsletter sign-up, social links
  • Booking system — Calendly or equivalent, linked from Beacons and LinkedIn CTA button
  • Rate card / pricing — clear pricing for 1:1, packages, and turnaround SLA. Have this ready before launch. People will ask.
  • LinkedIn DM template — a clear, warm response for inbound enquiries with a link to the booking page
  • 3–5 testimonials — from students placed at top universities. Short, specific, named where possible. On the landing page.
  • LinkedIn CTA banner — Canva banner for both profiles promoting the newsletter
  • Newsletter 'about' section — short bios, link to Loulou Learning, clear value prop
  • TikTok bio link — update to point to Beacons page (link in bio → all channels)

Quarterly review triggers

Is the fortnightly cadence sustainable? If quality dips, go monthly — better a slower excellent newsletter than a fast mediocre one. · Are readers converting to clients? If not, adjust where and how the CTA appears. · At 2,000+ subscribers: evaluate Substack / Beehiiv migration for list ownership. · At 5,000+ subscribers: consider a paid tier or first sponsorship. · Are both authors still enjoying it? If one isn't, the project dies. Check in quarterly.
